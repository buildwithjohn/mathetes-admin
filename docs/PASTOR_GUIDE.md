# Mathetes — Pastor's Guide

A plain, practical guide to the Mathetes admin dashboard. No technical jargon.
If you can use WhatsApp, you can use this.

The dashboard lives at **https://admin.mathetes.live**. The app your disciples
use is the Mathetes mobile app. Whatever you publish here, they see there.

---

## Signing in

1. Open **https://admin.mathetes.live** in any browser (phone or computer).
2. Enter your email and password and tap **Sign in**.
3. You land on the **Dashboard**.

Only you and your content team can sign in here. If you forget your password or
get locked out, contact the engineer (see the last section).

---

## Publishing a devotional

1. In the left menu, tap **Devotionals**.
2. Tap **New devotional** (top right).
3. Fill in the title, write the body, and add any scripture references.
   - Use the **Scripture block** button in the editor for verses — they show
     with the oxblood border in the app.
   - Reading time fills in automatically; you can override it.
   - You can attach an **audio narration** by pasting a link.
4. Pick a **publish date**.
5. Choose what to do:
   - **Save draft** — keep working, nobody sees it yet.
   - **Schedule** — it publishes itself on the date you chose.
   - **Publish now** — it goes live immediately.

The preview on the right shows how it will look on a disciple's phone.

---

## Scheduling the Word of the Day

1. Tap **Word of the Day**.
2. You see a calendar. Tap any future day.
3. Enter the verse reference, the verse text, a short reflection, and an
   optional prompt question.
4. **Schedule** it for that day, or **Publish** if it is for today.

You can only have one Word of the Day per day — the calendar stops you from
double-booking. Tip: you can fill a whole month in one sitting by tapping
through the days.

---

## Posting an announcement

1. Tap **Announcements** then **New announcement**.
2. Write the title and message.
3. Optional extras:
   - A **banner** (Event or Urgent) to make it stand out.
   - **Event details** (date, time, location) for gatherings.
   - **Photos**.
4. **Post now** or **Schedule** it.

When you publish, every member of the parish gets a notification.

---

## Answering Ask Pastor (public vs private)

1. Tap **Ask Pastor**. You see the queue of questions.
2. Use the filters: **Awaiting**, **Answered**, **Public**, **Private**.
3. Tap a question to open it, then write your response.
4. Choose how to answer:
   - **Public, anonymized** — your answer is added to the public Q&A feed so the
     whole parish can learn from it. The asker's name is never shown.
   - **Private** — only the person who asked sees your reply.
5. Tap send.

Ask Pastor is a queue, not a chat. Answer on your own rhythm; there is no
pressure-badge counting down at you.

---

## Managing members (house, campus, role)

1. Tap **Members**. Members are grouped by Leadership, Disciplers, and Members.
2. Tap **Edit** on anyone to change their:
   - **Role** (member, discipler, house leader, admin, pastor)
   - **House** fellowship
   - **Campus** (Oye or Ikole)
3. Save.

Changing a role changes what that person can see and do. Promote house leaders
and disciplers here.

---

## Reviewing moderation reports

1. Tap **Moderation**.
2. The top section is **reports** raised by members. For each you can:
   - **Review** — mark that you are looking into it.
   - **Resolve** — you have handled it.
   - **Dismiss** — no action needed.
3. The lower section, **Automated flags**, lists messages the system caught
   automatically.

Most of pastoral care still happens person to person. This page is just to make
sure nothing serious slips through.

---

## Reading analytics

1. Tap **Analytics**.
2. You see, at a glance:
   - How many members you have, and how many were active in the last 7 days.
   - Engagement broken down by type.
   - How much content you have published.
   - Members by campus.

Use it to notice trends, not to chase numbers. A quiet week is not a failure.

## When to call the engineer

Call the engineer for **system problems** only — things that are broken, not
decisions:

- You cannot sign in, or a page shows an error and will not load.
- Something you published is not appearing in the app at all.
- A member reports the app crashing or losing data.
- Anything that looks like a security or privacy problem.

---

## When NOT to call the engineer

These are **yours**, pastor — they are ministry and content decisions, not
technical ones:

- What to write in a devotional or Word of the Day.
- Whether to answer a question publicly or privately.
- Who to promote to house leader or discipler.
- How to handle a pastoral or disciplinary matter raised in a report.
- When to post an announcement.

The engineer keeps the tool running. The ministry is yours to lead.
